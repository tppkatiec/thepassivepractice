# The Private Practice Pro — Brand Guidelines

## Audience & purpose
TPPP serves private-practice professionals (e.g. therapists, coaches, consultants) who want to run a polished, sustainable business. The brand should feel **credible, calm, editorial, and quietly energetic** — not corporate, not cute.

## Personality in one line
> A modern editorial magazine for the business side of private practice — warm lavender grounded in slate, with a lime-bright pulse.

## Color system

Primary usage is **60 / 30 / 10**:
- **60%** — the two purples together (`#6772c3` background + `#8890cf` primary elements)
- **30%** — neutrals (`#fafafb` surface + `#3a506a` deep ink)
- **10%** — pops (`#daff00` lime, `#dcbed7` blush) reserved for CTAs, highlights, and emotional moments

| Color | Hex | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|---|
| BG (`#6772c3`) | Page canvas, hero sections | Fill tiles on a neutral canvas |
| Primary (`#8890cf`) | Subtle shapes, secondary buttons, accents on bg | Use for body copy |
| Ink (`#3a506a`) | Body text on light surfaces, grounding details | Use as a primary background (too heavy) |
| Surface (`#fafafb`) | Cards, long-form copy bg, text on `bg` | Pair with lime in large areas |
| Lime (`#daff00`) | CTA buttons, highlight underlines, eyebrow labels | Use on more than ~10% of a screen |
| Blush (`#dcbed7`) | Pullquotes, soft callouts, decorative | Mix with lime in the same element |

### Contrast notes
- On `#6772c3` bg: use `#fafafb` for text (passes AA for body).
- Lime `#daff00` is high-value — always pair lime **fill** with `#3a506a` ink, never white.
- Blush on purple bg reads decorative, not legible — don't use blush for text blocks.

## Typography

```
Headers      →  Instrument Serif, regular/italic, letter-spacing -0.02em, tight leading
Subheaders   →  Clash Display, bold (700), UPPERCASE, letter-spacing 0.16em
Body         →  Inter, 400/500, normal tracking, 1.55 leading
```

- **Never mix** Instrument Serif and Clash Display in the same line.
- **Subheader** always acts as an eyebrow *above* a serif header, not as a standalone title.
- Italics in Instrument Serif are a feature — use them for pullquotes and emphasis.
- Inter bold is acceptable for inline emphasis in body copy; avoid Clash inside paragraphs.

### Font sources
- **Instrument Serif** — Google Fonts: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Instrument+Serif
- **Inter** — Google Fonts: https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter
- **Clash Display** — Fontshare (free for commercial): https://www.fontshare.com/fonts/clash-display

## Voice & tone
Refer to `brand/voice.pdf` (upload when ready). Summary rules of thumb:

- **Direct, never flowery.** Say the thing.
- **Warm, not saccharine.** Respect the reader's expertise.
- **Editorial rhythm.** Short sentences next to long ones.
- **Confidence without jargon.** We translate business to practitioners, not the other way around.

## Layout principles
- Generous left/right gutters. The design should feel **airy on a purple canvas**.
- Editorial asymmetry > perfect centering.
- One serif headline per section. Don't stack two displays.
- Lime shows up once or twice per screen — it's the full stop, not the sentence.

## Don't
- Don't use pastel gradients.
- Don't drop shadows on the purple bg — use subtle ink-tinted shadows only on light surfaces.
- Don't round corners aggressively (pill for CTAs only; otherwise `--radius-md` or `--radius-lg`).
- Don't use stock-photo flourishes — prefer editorial imagery or solid color blocks.
